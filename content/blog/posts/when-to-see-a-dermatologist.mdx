People put off dermatology visits longer than they should. Often the trigger is finally getting tired of guessing — months of failed over-the-counter products, or a mole that's been changing, or a rash that won't quit.

Here are the most common reasons it's worth booking — and what to expect when you do.

## 1. A skin spot that's changing

Any mole that's growing, changing color, itching, or bleeding deserves evaluation. The good news: most are benign. The other news: this is the highest-value reason to come in.

## 2. Acne that isn't responding to over-the-counter

If 12 weeks of consistent OTC products haven't moved the needle, prescription therapy is **dramatically more effective**. Most patients wish they had come in sooner.

## 3. A rash that won't go away

Persistent, recurring, or strange-looking rashes are often misdiagnosed by general practitioners. Dermatologists train for four years in nothing but skin — diagnosis is faster and treatment more targeted.

## 4. Hair loss

Hair loss is a clinical condition that responds well to early intervention. Waiting until it's advanced makes regrowth harder. Modern dermatology has effective tools — but they work best early.

## 5. Cosmetic concerns

Pigmentation, fine lines, scarring, or texture issues — these are dermatology's home turf. Cosmetic dermatology can produce real, lasting improvement without surgery.

## 6. Annual skin cancer screening

In Texas, with our sun exposure, **yearly full-body screenings** are recommended for most adults. Twenty minutes a year, often insurance-covered, and the single best thing you can do for long-term skin health.

## What to expect at your first visit

- 45–60 minutes for new patients
- A full skin exam (if appropriate to your concern)
- A clear discussion of diagnosis and treatment options
- A written care plan you can take home
- Follow-up scheduled if needed

## How to prepare

- Bring a list of all current medications
- Come with clean skin, ideally no makeup
- Bring photos of past skin issues if relevant
- Write down your questions before you arrive
