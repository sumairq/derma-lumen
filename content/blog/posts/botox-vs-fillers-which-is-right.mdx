"Should I get Botox or filler?" is one of the most common questions in cosmetic dermatology — and one of the most misunderstood. They do **completely different things**, and the right choice depends on what's bothering you.

## The short answer

- **Botox softens lines caused by movement.** Frown lines, forehead lines, crow's feet.
- **Filler replaces volume that has been lost.** Cheeks, under-eyes, smile lines, lips.

Most patients in their 30s and 40s end up using both — for different reasons, in different areas.

## How Botox works

Botox (and its cousins Dysport, Xeomin, and Daxxify) are neuromodulators. They temporarily reduce the contraction of specific muscles. When you stop scrunching your forehead, the lines on it relax and fade.

**Best for:**
- Forehead lines
- Frown lines between the brows ("11s")
- Crow's feet
- Lip flip
- Jaw slimming (masseter)

Botox doesn't add volume — it reduces motion.

## How filler works

Dermal fillers (Juvederm, Restylane, RHA) are gels that physically sit under your skin to restore volume that has been lost — or to subtly enhance contour.

**Best for:**
- Under-eye hollows (tear troughs)
- Sunken cheeks
- Smile lines (nasolabial folds)
- Marionette lines
- Lips
- Chin and jawline contour

Filler doesn't change muscle movement — it changes volume.

## When you need both

Around the eyes is a classic example: Botox can soften crow's feet, while a touch of filler can lift the brow or restore under-eye volume. Working together, they produce results that neither could alone.

A skilled dermatologist will assess your whole face and recommend the right mix — not just whatever you walked in asking for.

## What I tell patients

**Start with Botox** if your concern is movement-based — lines that appear when you smile, frown, or raise your brows.

**Start with filler** if your concern is volume — hollow under-eyes, sunken cheeks, deeper folds at rest.
